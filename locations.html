There are six locations for the normal rally mode. Each location has 12 stages.

  1. Argolis, Greece
  2. Powys, Wales
  3. Monte Carlo, Monaco
  4. Baumholder, Germany
  5. Jämsä, Finland
  6. Värmland, Sweden

Greece has all variants of gravel, and not much more. It has stages over two routes, near the villages of Gimno and Platani in Argolis. Here is a video of a Ford Escort Mark II going through the Perasma Platani track.

Wales is made up of wet dirt going through a dense forest. Wales has stages over two routes in and around the Sweet Lamb offroad complex near Llangurig, Wales.

Monaco takes place on a mountain, where tarmac changes to ice as you get higher It has two routes up the Col de Turini in Alpes-Maritimes, France and is about 25km long.

Germany takes place on military training grounds, with stages over two routes near the town of Baumholder, Germany. It is famous for hinkelsteins; stones originally placed there to ensure tanks stay on the road. The stones still serve the same purpose.

Finland is made up of gravel, and famous for its many jumps and bumpy corners. The track is near the town Jämsa.It goes through Kontinjärv and Kakaristo.

Sweden is near Lysvik, Värmland. The track is made up of snow covered gravel.

Rallycross has just 3 tracks throughout europe, although Dirt Rally 2.0 expanded upon that with 10 extra tracks

The 3 tracks are:

  • Lydden Hill, England
  • Höljes, Sweden
  • Hell, Norway

In the hillclimb we go to Pikes Peak, an american strech of highway going up a mountain. The highway used to be tarmac and gravel, but in 2011 the gravel was patched up with asphalt.
